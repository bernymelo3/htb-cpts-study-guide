## Title
Section 36 — Beyond this Module

## Description
Next-step resources for deepening Active Directory enumeration and attack skills, including practice boxes, Pro Labs, video talks, and recommended blogs and researchers to follow.

## Tags
resources, practice, learning-path, community

## Key Concepts
- **Real-world applicability** – The techniques learned here are used daily by pentesters; mastering them is essential for team-based assessments that involve cross-domain pivoting, persistence, and hybrid cloud environments.
- **Further HTB modules** – BloodHound, LDAP, PowerView, and Cracking Passwords with Hashcat deepen specific skill areas.
- **Practice boxes** – Forest, Active, Reel, Mantis, Blackfield, Monteverde; the “Intro to Zephyr” track on the main HTB platform provides curated AD attack paths.
- **Pro Labs** – Dante (beginner/intermediate) and Offshore (advanced) offer large, realistic corporate networks with heavy AD exposure.
- **Endgames** – Ascension features two distinct AD domains for an extreme challenge.
- **Video talks** – “Six Degrees of Domain Admin” (BloodHound intro), “Designing AD DACL Backdoors”, “Kicking The Guard Dog of Hades” (original Kerberoasting release), “Kerberoasting 101” by Tim Medin.
- **Blogs & people to follow** – SpecterOps, Harmj0y, Sean Metcalf (AD Security), Shenaniganslabs, Dirk-jan Mollema, The DFIR Report, and 0xdf’s walkthroughs.
- **MITRE ATT&CK** – Use the framework to research attacks, TTPs, and corresponding defenses.

## Key Takeaways
- Active Directory is a vast, evolving field; continuous learning through labs, write‑ups, and community content is critical to staying sharp.
- Practice boxes from HTB (especially the Zephyr track) let you apply the module’s techniques in varied, realistic scenarios.
- Pro Labs and Endgames simulate full engagements with multiple domains and complex trust relationships, pushing your AD skills further.
- Following top security researchers and reading detailed attack walkthroughs (0xdf, SpecterOps, DFIR Report) bridges the gap between theory and real‑world tradecraft.
- The defensive side is equally important: understanding attacker TTPs through MITRE ATT&CK helps you both emulate threats and harden environments.
